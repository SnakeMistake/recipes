Shepherd's Pie

Winter Weather Comfort Food

This is a great meal when you need something warm and filling. Adapted from this NYTimes recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 package Beyond Meat
  • 3/4 cup French lentils
  • 3 1/2 cups vegetable broth
  • 8 ounces sliced mixed mushrooms
  • 1 large leek, white part only, thinly sliced
  • 1 to 2 tsp fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons flour
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 medium carrots
  • Olive oil or butter
  • 1/2 cup packed grated Parmigiano-Reggiano
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1 package Frozen Peas
  • 3-4 potatoes peeled and diced
  • 3 Tbsp tomato paste

Instructions:

  1. Boil the potatoes:In a large pot, boil enough heavily salted water to submerge the potatoes. Cook 15 to 20 minutes, until soft. Test with a fork to ensure they're cooked through.
  2. Prepare the filling:In a skillet or pan with tall sides, simmer the lentils, thyme and 2 cups of broth with 1 tsp salt. Cook about 20 minutes and then transfer to a bowl.
  3. Finish the filling: Heat the oven to 375 degrees. Melt butter in a skillet and cook mushrooms until golden brown (8-10 minutes). Reduce heat to medium and add the vegetables. Cook for another 10 minutes. Add tomato paste, sprinkle with flour, and then add the remaining broth, lentils, and peas. Cook until thick. Stir in lemon juice.
  4. Cook the pie:Place the mixture in a dutch oven. Top with mashed potatoes and spread evenly. Sprinkle with the Parmigiano. Transfer to the oven and bake until potatoes are browned and the edges are bubbling (approx. 30min.). Let stand at room temperature for 15 minutes before serving.
